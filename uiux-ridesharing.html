The Brief

Transportation is one of the senior community's biggest issues. Many cannot drive, but still desire and need to reach their communities. Seniors know about ridesharing solutions, but struggle navigating current user interfaces. The task: design a ridesharing app with accessibility at the forefront.

My Role - Explained

I worked closely with several senior groups to determine areas of unmet needs. Once determining transportation was a large issue, I created many visual designs and mockups in Adobe XD. The senior groups tested these designs over several rounds, which led to a more refined UI. I did not develop a backend for this app, but used existing platforms as a barometer for feasibility.

Summary

Shyft was designed for seniors, focusing on ease of use and accessibility by following WCAG standards. A straightforward, card-based homescreen clearly guides the user to specific tasks. Large text and use of contrast encourages quick, informed decisions with high accuracy. Accessibility settings are intuitive to find and change, ensuring trust that the driver will be able to cater to the user.

The Homescreen

Clean, large cards help minimize user error. Core functions are all displayed to minimize taps and frustration.

Talk or Text

Shyft provides users the flexibilty to type out destinations or go hands-free using natural language.

Accessibility

Accessibility accomodations are presented at the top of settings to give users the best chance of having their needs met.

Favorites

Research found that most users visit a consistent set of places. For this reason, favorites are prominantly displayed on the homescreen for quick access and editing.

Scheduling a ride

Seniors stated that they prefer to schedule their travel, and have a hard time doing this on current solutions. Scheduling is the default in Shyft.

Safety

A core value of Shyft is safety. Driver picture and vehicle are prominantly displayed with up-to-date information on their location and arrival time.
