The Brief

TranLoc Rider is a public transit app popular in cities and college campuses. It maps bus routes and tracks bus location, giving users more control over their public transportation experience. Through interviews and a survery, several of its features were found to be difficult to use and confusing. This project explored how applying principles of universal design and accessibility could enhance the user experience of TransLoc Rider.

This was a group project with 3 other graduate students at NC State. I designed the user interface for the onboarding process and was responsible for creating mid- and high-fidelity prototypes of the app's main functions.

Summary

The refresh of TransLoc Rider was centered around simplifying the user experience and improving education of features. Tabs were more clearly defined and only the most used ones were kept (5 to 3). Reachability was improved by moving these tabs from top to the bottom of the screen. An informative onboarding experience was developed to inform users of all primary features.

Onboarding

Clear and informative onboarding shows the users key features. It now includes route descriptions in setup.

Homescreen

The homescreen has been completely reconfigured. Reachability is improved with the tab bar, the search bar is more evident, and weather information is available at a glance.

Choosing Routes

By reducing screen count to find routes, adding a preview pane, and allowing for quick favoriting, users can more easily plan their trip.

Favorites

Before only one favorite route could be shown at once. Now with route groups, users can see multiple lines depending on the day's activities.
