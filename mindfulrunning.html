Did it work?

A skeptic's journey testing the Focus Pack

by Ryan Bolick
18 December 2019

The premise

Mindfulness is not something that came naturally to me. In fact, I was very skeptical until I started working on a project at North Carolina State University. I was managing a team with business and engineering students in a course on design engineering. Our initial objective was to help runners breathe better during running. However, we discovered that the problem was much more challenging than we originally thought. Our research uncovered that runners are not connecting to their mind, body, and surroundings, leading to dissatisfaction while training and even increased injury risk. Although we were skeptical, we set out to develop a mindful running solution.

The mindful running solution

Over the course of four months, the team and I built the Focus Pack platform. We integrated the Focus Pack into Strava, the leader in social fitness with over 48 million users. Strava is an expert at the data, but they do not currently have a mindful solution, which is why we thought they were a perfect fit for our mindful running solution.

You can read more about the Focus Pack that we developed here.

Case study: Pre-mindful running

Back in September 2019 when I started this project, I had been in a slump. Over the last 11 months my weekly mileage had plummeted from 42 miles a month to just 27.

Before that slump, I competed in over 15 races during the 2018 Spartan Race series. It was a grueling schedule, but I wanted to be in the top 10 of my age group and qualify for the world championship. My training was just enough to allow me to eek out a top 10 finish- beating the next racer by just 3 minutes in a 3 hour race. I had done it! I qualified for the world championship!

I was always proud to be race-motivated and centered my running around them. So when the season ended, my training did too, initiating an 11 month fitness slump. I couldn’t find the motivation to run because the races were many months away. However, when the races were coming up, I just found new excuses not to run.

My lack of training was not only a result of procrastination and excuses. I had tried training plans, new shoes, and even living with my amazing ultra running wife, Brinnae, didn’t help. It was time for something different.

Case study: Running with the Focus Pack

Here’s where mindfulness entered the picture. I’ll be honest, I entered as a huge skeptic, having tried many other methods. I had low expectations for the process.

As I built the Focus Pack I tried the prototypes and took in the principles of mindfulness that are manifested in the Mindful Goals feature. I thought my fitness would stabilize or with some luck improve slightly, but I was not prepared for what actually happened.

I went from running 27 miles a month to 78 almost overnight. Mindful running allowed me to do this without injury. I listened to my body and stopped worrying if I was running fast enough or far enough. Over a 3 month period, I ran 235 miles. That is only 59 miles less than my entire 7 month season and 67 miles less than my 11 month slump.

**The mileages above are estimates (+/- 2%). I am currently learning Python, which will enable me to connect to Strava’s API and run statistical analyses.

But wait, there's more

Over the course of four months, the team and I built the Focus Pack platform. We integrated the Focus Pack into Strava, the leader in social fitness with over 48 million users. Strava is an expert at the data, but they do not currently have a mindful solution, which is why we thought they were a perfect fit for our mindful running solution.

I’ve gone from a race-motivated “runner” who did only the bare minimum to increasing my Strava-calculated fitness score by 180% in just three months. I can now call myself a runner. I don’t just do it for the races anymore, running allows me to connect to nature, improve my fitness, and feel more energy.

Conclusions

Originally a skeptic, working on this project and integrating it into my own running during this case study has allowed me to become a more mindful, and thus, better. runner. Originally someone who could barely run a 3 mile training run, I am now feeling so good I may train for a marathon! I encourage everyone to try out mindful running.

I would like to give a shout out to my team of MBA and MS Engineering colleagues at North Carolina State University.
